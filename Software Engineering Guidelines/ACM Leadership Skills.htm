Motivational Communication

Phrases like:

I like that!
Go ahead…try it.
This is going to be fun!
How can I help?
I agree.
That's a great idea.
How can we make it work?

Avoid phrases like:

The problem with idea is…
It's not in our budget.
We've tried that before.
It'll never work.
Why start anything now?
It is not a bad idea but…


Sample format for Meetings

Call to order
Minutes of previous meeting
Reports of Officers
Reports of Standing Committees
Reports of Special Committees

Discussion of Old Business
Discussion of New Business
Announcements
Adjournment


Dealing with Difficult Members

Talking for the sake of being heard.
Conducting side conversations.
Challenging attempts to move the
group towards decisions.
Interpreting criticism of ideas
and personal attacks.
Waving off or negating all
suggestions or new ideas from others.
Urging the group to take action before
a problem is clearly identified.

Listen, do not debate.
Talk privately with members
who continuously exhibit
disruptive behavior.
Turn negative behaviors into
positive contributions.
Encourage the group to share the
responsibility of handling
difficult members.
